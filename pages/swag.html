Swag

Rules of thumb for collecting swag

  • Recruiters don't care about their swag
  • The first thing to grab is a bag to keep everything in
  • You can never have too many water bottles, headphones, charger banks, fidget spinners, mints, and thumb drives

There are three generally accepted methods to approach a recruiter's booth at the career fair and to leave with their swag

The Assassin

Wait in line. When your turn comes, smile and greet the recruiter. Learn a little bit about their company and act interested. Just as they begin to trust you, ask them for the swag that you want. They'll respond with, "Sure! Take whatever you want!" Mission accomplished.

The Robber

Lines looking a little long? Wait until recruiters are all busy to invade the booth. Grab a handful of swag and get out of there. Sort out what is actually worth keeping later.

The Vulture

Hover up and down the aisles, looking for specific swag you want. Once you have decided what you want, approach the recruiter. Cut to the chase: "Can I have that ..." Once you've feasted on your prize, retreat back to the skies to find your next meal.
